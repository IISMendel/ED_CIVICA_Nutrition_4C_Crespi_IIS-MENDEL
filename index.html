What is Nutrition?

Cosa si intende per nutrizione?

Nutrition is the series of processes by which your body takes in and uses nutrients for survival and growth. It is the study of how the body processes food to sustain life and prevent malnutrition — which can mean eating too little or too much of the wrong things.

Key Definition: Malnutrition encompasses both undernourishment (lack of food) and overnourishment (excessive or unbalanced consumption). This double challenge is at the heart of SDGs 2 and 3.

The Nutrient Cycle connects three levels: Plants generate carbohydrates via photosynthesis → Animals consume plants to build proteins and fats → Humans depend entirely on plant and animal agricultural outputs for survival and growth.

"Understanding nutrition transforms eating from a biological necessity into an act of civic responsibility." — Farm-to-Health Blueprint, IIS Mendel 2026
Agricultural Connection: Every nutrient in your body originates from either a plant or an animal raised in agriculture. The health of the soil directly determines the nutritional quality of the food.

The 6 Essential Nutrient Classes

Le 6 classi essenziali di nutrienti

Macronutrient
🌾

Carbohydrates

Primary fuel source. Natural compounds of carbon, hydrogen and oxygen. Excess is stored as glycogen in liver and muscles. Carboidrati: fonte primaria di energia.

  • Simple sugars → fast energy, little value
  • Complex carbs → slower release, rich in fibre
  • Energy: 16 kJ / 4 Cal per gram
Macronutrient
🥩

Proteins

Biological building blocks. Large molecules built from chains of amino acids. Essential for bones, muscles, cartilage, skin, and enzymes. Proteine: mattoni biologici.

  • 20 amino acids needed (9 essential)
  • Complete: meat, fish, eggs, dairy
  • Incomplete: beans, peas, nuts, seeds
Macronutrient
🫒

Lipids (Fats)

Dense energy and insulation. Supplies slow-release energy, insulates the body from toxins, and transports fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K). Lipidi: energia densa e isolamento.

  • Unsaturated: heart-healthy (olive oil, fish)
  • Saturated: limit (butter, red meat)
  • Trans: avoid (margarine, fried foods)
Micronutrient
🥬

Vitamins

Organic metabolic regulators. Needed in tiny amounts to regulate metabolism. Vitamine: regolatori metabolici organici.

  • Fat-soluble (A,D,E,K): stored in body fat
  • Water-soluble (C, B-complex): daily supply needed
  • Vitamin A → night vision & bone growth
  • Vitamin K → blood clotting
Micronutrient
💎

Minerals

Inorganic structural elements. Major minerals (>5g) vs trace minerals (<5g). Minerali: elementi strutturali inorganici.

  • Major: Calcium (bones, teeth, nerves)
  • Trace: Iron, zinc, selenium, copper, iodine
  • Sources: leafy greens, dairy, sardines
Foundation
💧

Water / Fibre / Oxygen

Essential for digestion and survival. Not always classified as standard nutrients but absolutely critical. Acqua e fibra: fondamentali per la vita.

  • Soluble fibre → lowers cholesterol
  • Insoluble fibre → digestive health
  • Water: 0 kJ, but essential for all reactions

The Physics of Food: Energy Values

L'energia degli alimenti

Food energy is measured in Calories (Cal/kcal) and kilojoules (kJ). The key conversion: 1 Calorie = 4.184 kJ. Weight gain occurs when energy input from food exceeds energy used through metabolism and exercise. Excess energy is stored by the body as fat.

Lipids: Storage & Insulation

I lipidi: non tutti i grassi sono uguali

🫒🐟

Unsaturated Fats ✅

Liquid at room temperature. Found in plant foods and fish. Heart-healthy.

Olive oil, peanut oil, canola oil, salmon, tuna

🧈🥩

Saturated Fats ⚠️

Solid at room temperature. Can raise blood cholesterol and heart disease risk. Limit intake.

Butter, cheese, meat, palm and coconut oils

🍩🍟

Trans Fats ❌

Created via industrial hydrogenation. Increase heart disease risk significantly. Avoid entirely.

Margarine, commercial baked goods, fried foods

Civic Guidelines: MyPlate & 5-A-DAY

Linee guida civiche per una dieta equilibrata

🍽 The MyPlate Standard (USDA)

Launched by the USDA to provide science-based, actionable dietary advice for citizens.

Fruits & Veggies: Make half your plate fruits and vegetables. Focus on whole fruits and vary your veggies.
Grains: Make at least half your grains whole grains for fibre and sustained energy.
Protein: Vary your protein routine — lean meat, fish, eggs, beans, nuts.
Dairy: Move to low-fat or fat-free milk/yogurt. Choose calcium-rich alternatives.
The Constraint: Choose foods and beverages with less added sugars, saturated fat, and sodium.

🍎 The 5-A-DAY Campaign

Launched by governments in English-speaking countries (US, UK) to improve general population health.

🍎 🥕 🍇 🫑 🥦
5 × 80g

Consume 5 distinct portions of fruits and vegetables daily. Standard portion = exactly 80 grams.

❤️ Heart

Promotes cardiovascular health

🦠 Gut

Improves bowel function via fibre

⚖️ Weight

Low calorie, high nutrient density

EU Consumer Literacy: Under EU regulations, all pre-packed foods must display a Nutrition Declaration. Energy is shown in both kJ and kcal per 100g. Always check: Total Fat → Saturated Fat; Carbohydrates → Sugars; Protein; Salt/Sodium.

The 5 Major Food Groups (Agricultural Outputs)

I 5 principali gruppi alimentari

🍎

Fruit

Rich in vitamins, minerals, and natural sugars. Serving: 1 medium apple/banana, or 1 cup canned fruit.

🥦

Vegetables & Legumes

Micronutrient powerhouse. Serving: ½ cup cooked vegetables or 1 cup salad. Legumes also provide plant protein.

🌾

Breads, Cereals, Rice, Pasta

Primary carbohydrate source. Choose wholegrain. Serving: 1 slice bread (40g) or ½ cup cooked rice/pasta.

🍗

Lean Meat, Poultry, Fish, Eggs

Complete protein sources. Serving: 65g cooked lean meat, 80–100g fish fillet, or 2 large eggs.

🥛

Milk, Yogurt, Cheese

Essential calcium and protein. Serving: 1 cup (250ml) milk or 2 slices (40g) cheese. Choose low-fat options.

⚠️

Extra Foods (Limit)

Lollies, soft drinks, fast food — provide high concentrated energy/sugar but are not essential to the diet. Minimise consumption.

Nutrition & the UN Agenda 2030

Nutrizione e Agenda 2030 — ogni SDG è collegato al cibo

"Food is not just a personal choice — it is a global political act. Every meal connects us to farmers, ecosystems, and the 17 Sustainable Development Goals." — Farm-to-Health Blueprint, IIS Mendel 2026

Nutrition is the thread that runs through all 17 SDGs. Below you can explore how every single goal of the UN Agenda 2030 connects to what we eat, how we grow food, and the choices we make at the table.

La nutrizione è il filo che attraversa tutti e 17 gli SDG. Esplora i collegamenti qui sotto.

1 No Poverty
Nessuna povertà

Nutrition Link

Malnutrition costs economies up to 2-3% of GDP through reduced productivity and increased healthcare costs. Food insecurity is both a cause and consequence of poverty — hungry people cannot work, study, or escape poverty traps.

Food security = economic security
2 Zero Hunger
Fame zero

Nutrition Link

The most direct SDG connection. SDG 2 calls for ending all forms of malnutrition by 2030: stunting, wasting, obesity and micronutrient deficiencies. Sustainable agriculture and fair food systems are the central tools.

Most direct connection ⭐
3 Good Health & Well-being
Salute e benessere

Nutrition Link

Diet is the single biggest risk factor for preventable disease worldwide. Cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, certain cancers, and obesity are all strongly nutrition-related. A healthy diet prevents illness and reduces healthcare system costs.

Diet = primary prevention
4 Quality Education
Istruzione di qualità

Nutrition Link

Malnutrition in children, especially iron and iodine deficiency, directly impairs cognitive development, concentration, and school performance. School feeding programmes improve attendance and learning outcomes. Hunger means an empty mind.

Hungry children can't learn
5 Gender Equality
Uguaglianza di genere

Nutrition Link

Women and girls bear a disproportionate burden of malnutrition. Anaemia affects 40% of pregnant women globally. Women grow 60-80% of the world's food in developing countries yet often have the least food security. Empowering female farmers improves household nutrition.

Women = food security key
6 Clean Water & Sanitation
Acqua pulita e igiene

Nutrition Link

Water is an essential nutrient. Agriculture uses 70% of global freshwater. Lack of clean water causes diarrhoea which prevents nutrient absorption even when food is available. Food contamination from unsafe water is a major malnutrition driver globally.

Water = the invisible nutrient
7 Affordable & Clean Energy
Energia pulita e accessibile

Nutrition Link

The food system consumes about 30% of global energy. From tractors to refrigeration to cooking, energy access determines food availability, preservation, and preparation. Renewable energy in agriculture reduces emissions and lowers food costs.

Food systems are energy-hungry
8 Decent Work & Growth
Lavoro dignitoso

Nutrition Link

The agri-food sector is the world's largest employer. Decent wages for farm workers and fair value chains are essential for food security. Malnourished workers are less productive — nutrition and economic growth are mutually reinforcing.

Agriculture = world's #1 employer
9 Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure
Innovazione e infrastrutture

Nutrition Link

Precision agriculture, food technology, biofortification, and cold-chain infrastructure are innovations that directly improve the nutritional quality and availability of food. Smart farming reduces waste and increases nutrient density of crops.

AgriTech improves nutrition
10 Reduced Inequalities
Ridurre le disuguaglianze

Nutrition Link

Food deserts in low-income neighbourhoods mean poor people eat poor diets. Wealthy populations overconsume; poor populations underconsume. Nutritional inequality mirrors economic inequality. Universal access to nutritious food is a matter of justice.

Food justice = social justice
11 Sustainable Cities
Città e comunità sostenibili

Nutrition Link

Urban farming, community gardens, rooftop agriculture and peri-urban food systems bring nutritious food closer to city residents. The Milan Urban Food Policy Pact connects cities around food sustainability strategies and reducing urban food deserts.

Milan leads on urban food policy
12 Responsible Consumption
Consumo e produzione responsabili

Nutrition Link

One-third of all food produced is wasted. Sustainable dietary patterns (reducing meat, choosing local and seasonal produce) lower both environmental footprint and chronic disease risk. Reading nutrition labels is an act of responsible consumption.

Sustainable diets = responsible choice
13 Climate Action
Azione per il clima

Nutrition Link

Agriculture and food systems account for 26% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Livestock is a major driver. Shifting to more plant-based diets can reduce food system emissions by up to 70%. Climate change also threatens crop yields and nutritional content of plants.

Diet change = climate action
14 Life Below Water
La vita sott'acqua

Nutrition Link

Overfishing threatens global fish stocks, a key protein and omega-3 source for billions. Ocean acidification reduces shell-building minerals available in seafood. Sustainable aquaculture is essential to maintain fish as a nutritious, accessible food source.

Fish = nutrition from the sea
15 Life on Land
La vita sulla terra

Nutrition Link

Healthy soil is the foundation of all nutrition. Soil biodiversity, organic matter, and soil nutrient density index directly determine the vitamins and minerals in our crops. Deforestation for agriculture destroys ecosystems that sustain nutritional diversity.

Soil health = human health
16 Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions
Pace, giustizia e istituzioni

Nutrition Link

Food insecurity is one of the leading drivers of conflict and political instability. Wars disrupt food systems and cause famine. Strong institutions — food safety agencies, agricultural regulators, trade bodies — are essential to guarantee nutritious food for all citizens.

Food security = geopolitical stability
17 Partnerships for the Goals
Partnership per gli obiettivi

Nutrition Link

Global nutrition challenges require international cooperation: the FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization), WHO, WFP, and IFAD work together on the UN Food Systems Summit agenda. Public-private partnerships fund biofortification and food fortification programmes worldwide.

FAO · WHO · WFP · IFAD
🌍 The Big Picture — Il quadro generale: No SDG can be fully achieved without addressing food and nutrition. SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) is the central hub, but nutrition intersects with poverty (1), health (3), education (4), climate (13), and ecosystems (14, 15). As a future agri-food professional, you are working on all 17 goals at once.

Explaining Nutrition Concepts

Spiegare i concetti di nutrizione

  • There are six essential classes of nutrients: carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, vitamins, minerals, and water. Each one plays a distinct and irreplaceable role in the body.
    Esistono sei classi essenziali di nutrienti, ognuna con un ruolo distinto e insostituibile.
    B2
  • Macronutrients — carbohydrates, proteins and fats — provide the energy that fuels all biological processes. They are measured in kilocalories or kilojoules per gram.
    I macronutrienti forniscono l'energia che alimenta tutti i processi biologici, misurata in kilocalorie o kilojoule.
    B2
  • Not all carbohydrates are equal. Simple sugars provide a rapid but unsustainable energy spike, whereas complex carbohydrates release energy gradually and are rich in dietary fibre — which is where agricultural choices really matter.
    Non tutti i carboidrati sono uguali: gli zuccheri semplici danno un picco energetico rapido, i carboidrati complessi rilasciano energia gradualmente.
    C1
  • Proteins are made of amino acids. The body requires 20 of them, and 9 are "essential" — meaning we cannot produce them ourselves; they must come from our diet. Animal products contain complete proteins; plant sources are typically incomplete, which is why vegetarians need to combine different plant foods.
    Le proteine sono formate da aminoacidi. Il corpo ne richiede 20, di cui 9 "essenziali" che devono arrivare dagli alimenti.
    C1
  • Contrary to popular belief, not all fats are harmful. Unsaturated fats — found in olive oil, nuts, and fish — are actually heart-healthy, whereas trans fats, created through industrial hydrogenation, should be avoided entirely.
    Contrariamente alla credenza popolare, non tutti i grassi fanno male. I grassi insaturi sono salutari per il cuore.
    C1
  • The EU requires all pre-packed food to display a Nutrition Declaration showing energy, fats, carbohydrates, protein and salt per 100g. This is an important example of civic regulation protecting public health.
    L'UE obbliga tutti gli alimenti confezionati a mostrare una dichiarazione nutrizionale per 100g.
    B2
  • The 5-A-DAY campaign recommends consuming five distinct portions of fruits and vegetables daily, each of 80 grams. This simple guideline, if universally followed, could dramatically reduce the burden of preventable disease.
    La campagna 5 al giorno raccomanda cinque porzioni da 80g di frutta e verdura al giorno.
    B2

📚 Key Vocabulary — Glossario essenziale

Macronutrient — macronutriente
Nutrient needed in large amounts for energy (carbs, proteins, fats)
Micronutrient — micronutriente
Nutrient needed in small amounts for regulation (vitamins, minerals)
Malnutrition — malnutrizione
Both under- and over-nourishment; imbalanced nutrient intake
Food System — sistema alimentare
The entire chain from farming to consumption and waste
Dietary Fibre — fibra alimentare
Plant parts that resist digestion; vital for gut health
Biofortification — biofortificazione
Increasing nutritional value of crops through breeding or agronomy
Food Security — sicurezza alimentare
When all people have access to sufficient, nutritious food at all times
Civic Responsibility — responsabilità civica
The duty of a citizen to contribute to the common good
